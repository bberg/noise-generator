# Audience Analysis

## Primary User Segments

### 1. Sleep Seekers (35% of users)

#### Demographics
- Age: 25-55
- Gender: Slightly female-skewed (55%)
- Lifestyle: Busy professionals, new parents, urban dwellers
- Tech comfort: Moderate

#### Pain Points
- Difficulty falling asleep
- Light sleepers disturbed by partner, neighbors, traffic
- Irregular sleep schedules (shift workers, travelers)
- Racing thoughts at bedtime

#### Use Context
- **When**: Bedtime (9pm-midnight), middle-of-night wake-ups
- **Where**: Bedroom, via phone speaker, Bluetooth speaker, or headphones
- **Duration**: All night (6-8 hours) or until asleep (15-60 min)
- **Device**: 60% mobile, 30% tablet, 10% laptop

#### What They Need
- Simple one-click start
- Sleep timer that fades out
- Consistent sound (no jarring changes)
- Works with screen off
- Gentle wake (alarm integration would be ideal)

#### What Would Make Them Return
- Reliable (never crashes or stops)
- Remembers their settings
- Works offline (phone on airplane mode)
- Free (no "trial expired" interruptions)

---

### 2. Focus Workers (30% of users)

#### Demographics
- Age: 20-45
- Occupation: Knowledge workers, students, creatives, developers
- Tech comfort: High
- Many with ADHD (diagnosed or suspected)

#### Pain Points
- Open office noise distractions
- Work-from-home interruptions (kids, pets, deliveries)
- Difficulty maintaining concentration
- Procrastination and task-switching

#### Use Context
- **When**: Work hours (9am-6pm), study sessions
- **Where**: Home office, library, coffee shop, coworking space
- **Duration**: Pomodoro sessions (25-50 min) or continuous (2-4 hours)
- **Device**: 70% desktop/laptop, 30% mobile

#### What They Need
- Quick access (bookmark or keyboard shortcut)
- Minimal visual distraction (plays in background tab)
- Timer integration
- Different modes for different tasks (deep work vs. light work)
- Research backing (proves it works)

#### What Would Make Them Return
- Noticeable productivity improvement
- Timer/Pomodoro features
- Saves their preferred mix
- Integrates with their workflow (browser extension)

---

### 3. Relaxation/Anxiety Relief (15% of users)

#### Demographics
- Age: All ages
- Mental health: Anxiety, stress, panic disorders
- Tech comfort: Varies widely

#### Pain Points
- Anxiety and racing thoughts
- Stress from work/life
- Need for grounding during panic attacks
- Meditation support

#### Use Context
- **When**: During stress episodes, meditation sessions, wind-down time
- **Where**: Anywhere (home, office, transit)
- **Duration**: 10-30 minutes typically
- **Device**: 50% mobile, 50% desktop

#### What They Need
- Calming interface (no harsh colors or animations)
- Immediate playback (low friction)
- Soothing sound options (brown noise, nature)
- Volume that stays consistent

#### What Would Make Them Return
- Reliably calming experience
- Modulation/oscillation features (mimics breathing)
- Integration with breathing exercises
- Trusted resource (linked to mental health information)

---

### 4. Tinnitus Sufferers (10% of users)

#### Demographics
- Age: 40+ primarily (but increasingly younger due to earbuds)
- Condition: Chronic tinnitus (ringing, buzzing, hissing)
- Tech comfort: Moderate
- Often frustrated with healthcare options

#### Pain Points
- Constant ringing/noise in ears
- Difficulty sleeping due to tinnitus
- Few effective treatments
- Expensive masking devices

#### Use Context
- **When**: Sleep time, quiet environments where tinnitus is noticeable
- **Where**: Bedroom, quiet office
- **Duration**: Extended (overnight, all-day background)
- **Device**: Split between desktop and mobile

#### What They Need
- Frequency customization to match their tinnitus
- High/low cut filters to target specific ranges
- Information about tinnitus masking
- Notched audio options (advanced)

#### What Would Make Them Return
- Actually helps (provides relief)
- Specific tinnitus guidance
- Free (medical costs are already high)
- Trustworthy information from medical sources

---

### 5. Parents of Babies/Children (5% of users)

#### Demographics
- Age: 25-40
- Life stage: New parents, parents of toddlers
- Sleep-deprived, desperate for solutions
- Tech comfort: High

#### Pain Points
- Baby won't sleep
- Baby wakes frequently
- Household noise disturbs baby
- Exhaustion from sleep deprivation

#### Use Context
- **When**: Nap time, bedtime, throughout night
- **Where**: Nursery, bedroom
- **Duration**: Hours at a time
- **Device**: Often dedicated tablet/old phone as "white noise machine"

#### What They Need
- Safe volume guidance
- Pink noise (gentler on developing ears)
- Reliable all-night playback
- Simple controls (can operate while exhausted)

#### What Would Make Them Return
- Baby sleeps better
- Works reliably
- Easy to share with partner/caregiver
- Research on safety for infant use

---

### 6. Audio Professionals/Scientists (5% of users)

#### Demographics
- Occupation: Sound engineers, researchers, audiologists, developers
- Education: Often technical/scientific background
- Tech comfort: Very high

#### Pain Points
- Need precise, measurement-grade noise
- Testing audio equipment
- Research requiring specific noise profiles
- Building audio applications

#### Use Context
- **When**: Work hours, testing sessions
- **Where**: Studios, labs, offices
- **Duration**: Short bursts for testing, longer for calibration
- **Device**: Desktop with professional audio setup

#### What They Need
- Exact spectral specifications
- Algorithm documentation
- High-quality audio output
- Export capabilities
- Precise frequency control

#### What Would Make Them Return
- Professional-grade accuracy
- Transparent methodology
- Technical documentation
- Free for research/education use

## User Journey Maps

### Sleep Seeker Journey
```
Trigger: Can't sleep, lying awake
   |
Search: "white noise for sleep" / "sleep sounds"
   |
Land: Homepage
   |
Action: Click "Sleep" preset -> Play
   |
Success: Falls asleep
   |
Return: Bookmarks page, returns nightly
   |
Advocate: Recommends to friends with sleep issues
```

### Focus Worker Journey
```
Trigger: Can't concentrate, distracted
   |
Search: "background noise for work" / "focus sounds"
   |
Land: Homepage
   |
Action: Try different mixes, find preference
   |
Success: Gets more done
   |
Return: Opens in browser tab daily
   |
Advocate: Shares with coworkers, posts on productivity forums
```

### Tinnitus Sufferer Journey
```
Trigger: Tinnitus unbearable, seeking relief
   |
Search: "tinnitus masking sounds" / "tinnitus relief"
   |
Land: Homepage (hopefully via tinnitus content)
   |
Action: Adjusts frequency filters, tests different noises
   |
Success: Finds relief
   |
Return: Uses regularly for relief
   |
Advocate: Shares in tinnitus support communities
```

## Retention Factors

### What Brings Users Back

| Factor | Importance | Our Current State |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| Reliability | Critical | Good |
| Remembered settings | High | Missing |
| Free access | High | Excellent |
| Works offline | High | Missing (PWA) |
| Fast load time | High | Good |
| Scientific credibility | Medium | Basic |
| Mobile experience | High | Needs work |
| Bookmark-ability | Medium | Good |

### What Drives Users Away

1. **Site doesn't work** (audio fails, crashes)
2. **Requires signup** (friction)
3. **Paywall appears** (bait and switch feeling)
4. **Settings lost** (have to re-configure every time)
5. **Poor mobile experience** (hard to use on phone)
6. **Audio quality issues** (clicks, pops, loops audible)
